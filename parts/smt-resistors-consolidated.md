## Description

SMT resistors are compact, surface-mounted components ideal for modern PCB designs. These resistors come in standard package sizes (0603, 0805, or 1206) and are suitable for a wide range of applications including signal conditioning, biasing, pull-up/pull-down networks, and current limiting.

- Signal conditioning and biasing
- Pull-up and pull-down networks
- Current limiting for LEDs
- Voltage dividers
- Impedance matching
- Sensor signal conditioning
- Audio circuits
- Power supply feedback networks
- General-purpose PCB design

## Standard Resistor Values

These are E12 and E24 series standard values commonly used in electronics:

- **47Ω**: Low impedance applications, current limiting
- **100Ω**: General purpose, impedance matching
- **470Ω**: LED current limiting, general purpose
- **1kΩ**: Pull-up/pull-down networks, general purpose
- **10kΩ**: High impedance applications, sensor biasing
- **10.2kΩ**: Precision applications, specific circuit requirements

## Installation

SMT resistors are designed for surface mounting on PCBs using standard SMT soldering techniques (reflow or wave soldering). They can also be hand-soldered for prototyping applications using a soldering iron.

## Storage

Store in a cool, dry location. SMT resistors are typically supplied in tape and reel format or in bulk containers. Keep organized by value to prevent mixing.
